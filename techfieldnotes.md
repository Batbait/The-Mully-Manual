# Technology Field Notes

This section serves as a living record of my experiences working in technology.

Unlike the other technology manuals, which focus on established knowledge and concepts, these field notes are intended to document ongoing learning, interesting discoveries, troubleshooting experiences, project updates, career development milestones, and observations from day-to-day work.

Technology changes far too quickly for any single person to keep up with everything.

The goal of these notes is not to document every new tool or trend.

Instead, I want to record things that taught me something useful.

## Why Keep Technology Notes?

One of the biggest lessons I've learned throughout my technology career is that knowledge fades surprisingly fast.

You may solve a difficult problem on Monday and completely forget the details six months later.

You may discover a useful tool, script, command, or troubleshooting technique only to find yourself searching for it again years later.

Documentation is one of the most valuable skills in technology.

These notes serve as documentation for myself as much as anyone else.

## A Personal Note

One of the reasons I created The Mully Manual was the realization that many lessons disappear if they aren't recorded.

This happens in hunting.

It happens in fishing.

It definitely happens in technology.

I've forgotten enough useful information over the years to realize that relying on memory alone is a poor strategy.

These notes are my attempt to fix that.
