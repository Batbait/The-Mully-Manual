# Fishing Manual

Fishing has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember.

Growing up along the North Branch of the Potomac River near Ridgeley, West Virginia, I spent countless hours exploring rivers, streams, ponds, and lakes throughout Appalachia. Over the years I've targeted everything from panfish and trout to bass, catfish, and whatever else happened to be willing to bite that day.

Like most anglers, I've had days where every cast seemed to produce a fish and plenty of days where I couldn't buy a bite. Both taught valuable lessons.

My time in the Marine Corps expanded my understanding of fishing beyond the freshwater environments I grew up with. While stationed at Camp Lejeune, North Carolina, I learned to fish saltwater and brackish environments, adapting to new species, tides, techniques, and challenges that were completely foreign to an Appalachian river fisherman.

This manual is my attempt to document the lessons, observations, successes, failures, and stories collected along the way.

I don't claim to know everything about fishing. Far from it. But every trip teaches something, and those lessons are worth preserving.

## Topics

### River Fishing
Observations, techniques, and lessons learned from fishing moving water.

### Lake & Pond Fishing
Strategies and experiences from still-water environments.

### Catfishing
Tactics, gear, and lessons from pursuing one of my favorite species.

### Seasonal Patterns
How weather, temperature, water conditions, and time of year affect fish behavior.

### Gear & Tackle
Equipment I've used, what worked, what didn't, and why.

### Fishing Reports & Field Notes
Current observations and lessons learned from recent trips.

## Philosophy

The best anglers aren't always the ones who catch the most fish.

They're the ones who keep learning.

Every river has something to teach. Every missed fish has a lesson behind it. Every successful trip is simply the result of knowledge gained from previous failures.

The goal of this manual isn't to prove how much I know.

The goal is to document what I've learned so the next person can learn a little faster.
