# About

Welcome to The Mully Manual

If you want a quick look at some of my past adventures, [Look no further!](beforemanual.html)

My name is Ethan, though most people online know me as Mully.


I created The Mully Manual to document and preserve knowledge I've collected throughout a lifetime spent outdoors, working with technology, and learning through experience. None of us gets to keep our knowledge forever, and I've always believed that lessons are worth sharing if they can help someone else avoid mistakes, discover a new skill, or simply enjoy life a little more or a little easier. 


I grew up along the North Branch of the Potomac River near Ridgeley, West Virginia. Some of my earliest memories are of fishing rivers, streams, ponds, and lakes throughout Appalachia. When I wasn't outside, I was usually playing video games or planning and prepping for the next adventure I was bound for.


As a kid, I was heavily involved in the Boy Scouts. While I never earned the rank of Eagle Scout, the values of self-reliance, outdoor skills, preparedness, and lifelong learning stayed with me. Those lessons helped shape who I became long after I stopped attending meetings.


Growing up in the Appalachian Mountains gave me opportunities to hunt and fish from an early age. Over the years I've pursued whitetail deer, turkey, bear, squirrel, and many other species throughout the region. Every season, every success, and every failure taught me something valuable about the outdoors, wildlife, and myself.


After high school, I joined the United States Marine Corps, serving four years as an Electro-Optical Ordnance Technician. That experience took me far from the mountains and rivers I knew. Stationed at Camp Lejeune, North Carolina, I had to learn entirely new environments, from fishing saltwater and brackish estuaries to navigating swamps, coastal forests, and pine barrens. I took up fur bearing animal trapping and got several seasons of experience in successes and failures.


The Marine Corps also gave me the opportunity to deploy to Norway, where I spent time exploring rugged landscapes, hiking through terrain unlike anything I'd seen back home, and fishing in fjords that looked like something out of a storybook. Hunting for reindeer sheds and trying not to become mosquito food will always be a highlight of that trip. 


After leaving the military, I returned to civilian life and worked as an explosives operator for Northrop Grumman while pursuing a degree and professional certifications in cybersecurity. Midway through that journey, I transitioned into the technology field with IBM, starting as a Help Desk Technician and eventually earning a promotion to Tier 2 support.


Today, I hold a Bachelor of Science in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance from Western Governors University and continue building my career in technology while staying connected to the outdoors.


One of the most meaningful milestones in my life was purchasing my childhood home from my mother and returning to the banks of the river where so many of these experiences began. Living here again has reminded me how much knowledge can be gained from paying attention to the world around us and how important it is to preserve that knowledge for the future.


That is what The Mully Manual is all about.


You'll find hunting stories, fishing lessons, technology projects, cybersecurity insights, gear reviews, outdoor observations, and whatever else I learn along the way. I don't claim to know everything, but I do believe that every experience contains a lesson worth sharing.


If something here helps you learn faster than I did, then The Mully Manual is doing exactly what it was created to do.
