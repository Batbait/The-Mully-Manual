# Homelabs & Projects

One of the best ways to learn technology is by building something.

Courses, books, certifications, and videos all have their place, but I've always found that real understanding comes from hands-on experience.

This section documents projects I've completed, lessons I've learned, mistakes I've made, and experiments I've tried over the years.

I don't consider myself a hardcore homelabber. In fact, I've become less interested in maintaining large homelab environments as I've gotten older.

Homelabs can become expensive quickly, both in terms of money and time.

My preference has always been practical projects that solve real problems, teach useful skills, or simply satisfy my curiosity.

## Philosophy

I believe the best projects do at least one of three things:

- Teach a useful skill
- Solve a real problem
- Make life more interesting

Ideally, they accomplish all three.

The goal isn't building the most impressive lab.

The goal is learning something useful.

## Projects

### Home Security Camera System

One of my first major projects involved installing RTSP and PoE cameras around my home.

The cameras were connected to my desktop computer for:

- Monitoring
- Recording
- Remote observation

This project taught me:

- Basic networking
- Power over Ethernet
- Camera configuration
- Video recording systems
- Cable management

Most importantly, it taught me that planning cable routes is usually more difficult than configuring the technology itself.

### Raspberry Pi Projects

Raspberry Pi devices introduced me to a world of inexpensive experimentation.

#### Pi-hole

I built a Pi-hole to improve network visibility and reduce unwanted advertising traffic.

Topics learned included:

- DNS
- Linux administration
- Network configuration
- Device management

#### Smart TV Conversion

Using a Raspberry Pi, I converted an older television into a functional smart TV.

While simple, the project demonstrated how inexpensive hardware can dramatically extend the usefulness of older technology.

#### Raspberry Pi Cases

After purchasing several Raspberry Pis, I designed and 3D printed custom cases.

This introduced me to:

- CAD software
- 3D printing
- Design iteration
- Problem solving

Like many projects, the final design looked much better than the first version.

### 3D Printing

3D printing became one of the more useful hobbies I've explored.

Being able to design and manufacture simple solutions at home is incredibly rewarding.

Projects included:

- Raspberry Pi cases
- Organizational accessories
- Equipment holders
- Small utility parts

3D printing taught me that designing a part is often more challenging than printing it.

### The Mully Manual

This website is arguably my largest project.

The goal is simple:

Document useful knowledge and lessons learned throughout life.

Building the site introduced me to:

- GitHub Pages
- Markdown
- Version control
- Static websites
- Content organization

More importantly, it gave me a place to preserve information that would otherwise eventually be forgotten.

## Lessons Learned

### Projects Teach Faster Than Reading

Reading helps.

Doing helps more.

Many concepts that seemed confusing in theory became obvious once I was forced to build something with them.

### Start Small

Some of my most successful projects started as simple ideas.

A project doesn't need to be complicated to be valuable.

### Failure Is Part Of The Process

Nearly every project I've completed required troubleshooting.

Mistakes aren't a sign of failure.

They're part of the learning process.

### Technology Doesn't Need To Be Expensive

One of the biggest misconceptions about home projects is that they require large budgets.

Some of the most educational projects I've completed involved inexpensive hardware and a willingness to experiment.

## Philosophy

I've reached a point where I'm less interested in building technology simply for the sake of having technology.

Instead, I prefer projects that serve a purpose.

Whether that purpose is learning, security, convenience, or simply curiosity, the most rewarding projects are usually the ones that solve a problem.

The goal of this manual isn't to show off a lab.

It's to document what I've built, what I've learned, and what I'd do differently next time.
